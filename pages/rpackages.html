mStats

Introduction

The package mStatscame into life when I started my PhD in 2018. The first version of mStats was started in late 2018. Initially it was intended for fun and I wanted to know how much I could make use of what I have learnt so far. Also I would like to try something different but may not be entirely innovative since there are tens of thousands of R packages out there.

There are two packages that deeply inspired me: GGPlot2 and dplyr. With that inspiration in my mind, I developed version 1 of mStats where I tried an easy way to make ready-made plots with GGPLOT2. It's a lot fun and this in turn encourages me to do more with the package. I updated several small patch versions with a few functions that were interesting to me at that time. It's still dependent on GGPLOT2.

In mid 2019, I started another package called stats2. In this package, I tried to simplify the concept that one function should do one job without too many options for users. For example, tab function should perform the task of tabulation with few options to tweak its outputs. That's the whole idea of the package. Although this may be limiting the advantages of R, I hardly doubt beginners would mind this. In fact, such consistency may make users' life easier to get their jobs in R done.

Keeping that in mind, I am constantly developing and upating functions. In early 2020, I decided that the package is mature enough to be standalong package for data manipulation and analysis. I submitted to CRAN and one month later after several revision, it was released on 31st March 2020 on its version 3.2.2.

As times progress, I hope this package will at least provide some contributions in some people. Any constructive criticism, suggestions or comemnts are most welcome. Please contact me at dr.myominnoo@gmail.com.

Overview

mStats is a tool for epidemiological data analysis, providing four consistent domains of functions that help you perform the most common procedures of data analysis flow.

  • data manipulation - helps you manipulate, process and manage your dataset.
  • statistical analysis - lets you create well-formated outputs of common statistical procedures.
  • epidemiological calculation - integrates such calculations into your pipeline of analysis.
  • miscellaneous - includes functions that support functions from the other three domains and streamline transfering results outside R environment.

Installation

The easiest way to get mStats is from CRAN.

install.packages("mStats")
Developmental version

To get a bug fix or to use a feature from the development version, you can install the development version of mStats from GitHub.

# install.packages("devtools")
devtools::install_github("myominnoo/mStats")

mStatslite


The package mStatsLite is an extension to mStats that offers two functions not available in mStats.

  • ilog - stores the outputs displayed in R console and save as .txt file. This can be processed further for the purpose of data transfer and creating publishable tables.
  • clear - is a quick function that removes all objects from Global environment, clear R plots and R console.

This package can be installed by running the following command in R console.


install.packages("https://myominnoo.github.io/pages/r/mStatsLite_1.0.0.tar.gz", repos = NULL, type = "source")
