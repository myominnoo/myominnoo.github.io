EpiData

Introduction

It first came to my knowledge when I was encouraged to apply for an international research training course. I didn't know at that time that this course would change my life. It was back in 2014. As I entered the application form for the training course, I was stuck with a question asking what my preliminary level of skill in Epidata was. I didn't know what it was. One of my seniors who recommended me to the course also haned over the training course material he received to me. They almost finished the course. So I thought to myself why not learn the whole book. It took me a few days to finish the books. Later, I came to realize that the training course was well-known as Structured Operational Research Training IniTiatives (SORT-IT) and I became an alumni as well as facilitator in several courses after that. The book I went through was one of a very few books on data collection using EpiData Entry.

Conventional way of teaching research methodology rarely includes discussion or demonstration of data collection process as well as data transcription from paper-based records to electronic ones. If you dig deep, you will find several books and papers discussing theoretical framewrok of data management and data warehousing which is far beyond what someone would need with a simple operational reserach. That's why perhaps non-researchers or non-academic staffs sometimes find themselves learning and doing research extremely intimidating and beyond their reach. These are the reasons why I am having this website, and constantly using my time to develop tools and guidebooks.

As a part of my hobby as well as a personal project, I am developing a guidebook to EpiData using EpiData Manager and EntryClient. As an initiatives, I created a few introductory videos on this topic and made them available on YouTube.
