Hi! I am Myo, a global health researcher and currently a Ph.D. student at the Epidemiology Unit under the Faculty of Medicine, Prince of Songkla University in Thailand. I conduct health research that aims to bring evidence-based policy and practice changes to improve the population health and health system. I have 5+ years of experience in global health delivery of infectious disease responses with a focus on Tuberculosis and HIV as well as with a keen interest in non-communicable diseases and aging. He has additional qualifications in epidemiological research as well as specialist skills in global health security and implementation research with excellent track records of publications in international peer-reviewed journals.

Current Works

My main work is my Ph.D. thesis project on access to health care, TB, and migration. I am also engaged with other additional tasks like developing an R package and its companion help book, writing a book on EpiData, peer-reviews, and collaborative research projects with peer researchers. For detail, check my CV.

  • Statistics with R is an online course where I teach statisics using R and mStats package. Recordings were serialized and uploaded on YouTube for free access. I initiated this course to make use of time during stay home period of COVID-19 pandemic in early 2020. Two courses (one in English and in Myanmar) were offered and about 150 people from different backgrounds participated in these courses. Participants joined from Bhutan, India, Pakistan, Nepal, China and Myanmar.

  • mStats is a R package used for epidemiological data analysis. This development was initiated as an attempt to make learning R as easy as possible and get users started with data analysis in R as easy as possible. The outputs largely mimics STATA's output and STATA users may find it very intuitive. It is available on CRAN and its development version can be installed from mStats repository on github.

  • A beginner guide to research data collection using EpiData teaches how to build data collection tools using EpiData software. The book is available on Amazon and Leanpub.
